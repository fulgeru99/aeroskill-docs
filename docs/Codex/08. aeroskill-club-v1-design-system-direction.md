# Aeroskill Club V1 Design System Direction

Document date: 2026-06-28  
Product: Aeroskill Club web platform  
Version: V1 design direction draft  
Brand input: Provided Aeroskill Club logo, dark navy wordmark with aircraft mark

## 1. Design Goal

Aeroskill Club should feel like a credible general aviation membership organization: modern, polished, operationally serious, and quietly aspirational.

The design should not feel like:

- A generic SaaS dashboard.
- A corporate association template.
- A decorative aviation fan site.
- A luxury travel brand.
- A military or airline brand.

The correct tone is:

- General aviation.
- Member-focused.
- Trusted.
- Clean.
- Practical.
- Premium without being glossy.
- Technical without feeling cold.

## 2. Brand Reading From Logo

The logo is strong, simple, and aviation-specific. It uses a dark navy wordmark, wide uppercase lettering, and an aircraft silhouette integrated between the words.

Design implications:

- The brand should be led by navy, white, and precise spacing.
- The aircraft symbol gives the brand enough aviation identity; the UI does not need repeated airplane decorations.
- The letterforms suggest confidence, clarity, and a technical club character.
- The platform should use strong alignment, clean geometry, and restrained contrast.
- The identity should feel closer to a premium flying club and aviation operations desk than to a startup landing page.

## 3. Design Principles

### 3.1 Aviation, Not Airline

Use general aviation references:

- Small aircraft.
- Aerodromes.
- Flight schools.
- Hangars.
- Pilot communities.
- Cockpit details.
- Route planning and local flying.

Avoid airline-style imagery:

- Commercial jet cabins.
- Airport terminals.
- Business-class travel.
- Generic clouds viewed from airliners.

### 3.2 Polished, Not Decorative

The UI should feel considered and high quality, but not overdesigned.

Use:

- Crisp typography.
- Strong grid alignment.
- Clear hierarchy.
- High-quality aviation photography.
- Subtle technical details.

Avoid:

- Decorative gradient blobs.
- Overlapping floating cards.
- Abstract tech illustrations.
- Excessive shadows.
- Repeated airplane icons as decoration.

### 3.3 Credible, Not Corporate-Generic

The site should make the club feel real and operational.

Use:

- Concrete benefit categories.
- Real partner/sponsor visibility.
- Member card preview.
- Aerodrome/flight school language.
- Practical calls to action.

Avoid:

- Vague business phrases.
- Stock handshake imagery.
- Generic "unlock your potential" copy.
- Empty dashboard-style metrics without context.

### 3.4 Member-First

The member portal should make the member feel recognized.

Always prioritize:

- Membership status.
- Valid-until date.
- Digital card.
- Eligible benefits.
- Renewal/payment status.

### 3.5 Admin Calm

The admin CRM should be dense, calm, and efficient.

Use:

- Tables.
- Filters.
- Clear status chips.
- Minimal chrome.
- Predictable detail tabs.

Avoid:

- Marketing-style cards in admin views.
- Large decorative headers.
- Heavy imagery.
- Oversized typography.

## 5. Color Direction

The logo establishes dark navy as the primary brand color. The broader palette should add sky, metal, paper, and a restrained signal accent so the interface does not become one-note blue.

Exact logo colors should be sampled from the source logo before final implementation. The values below are recommended working tokens.

### 5.1 Core Palette

| Token | Color | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Aeroskill Navy | Deep navy | `#072A4A` | Logo, primary headers, primary buttons, navigation |
| Cockpit Navy | Near-black blue | `#061A2F` | Dark surfaces, footer, premium member card |
| Sky Blue | Clear aviation blue | `#2D7FBF` | Links, selected states, secondary CTAs |
| Altitude Blue | Pale sky | `#DCECF7` | Soft backgrounds, info panels |
| Cloud White | Warm white | `#F8FAFC` | Page backgrounds |
| Hangar White | Pure surface | `#FFFFFF` | Cards, forms, tables |
| Runway Slate | Blue-gray | `#334155` | Body text, admin UI |
| Instrument Gray | Cool gray | `#64748B` | Secondary text, metadata |
| Panel Line | Border gray | `#D8E0E8` | Dividers, table borders |
| Signal Amber | Controlled accent | `#C98A1F` | Premium tier, warnings, renewal attention |
| Clearance Green | Success | `#1F8A5B` | Active/paid/valid states |
| Alert Red | Error | `#C2413A` | Failed/expired/destructive states |

### 5.2 Usage Ratio

Recommended visual balance:

- 45% white/light neutral.
- 25% navy.
- 15% blue-gray/slate.
- 10% sky blue.
- 5% amber/green/red status colors.

Rules:

- Navy should anchor the brand, not flood every section.
- Amber should be used sparingly for premium, warning, and renewal emphasis.
- Green and red should remain semantic, not decorative.
- Avoid full-page blue gradients.
- Avoid purple, beige, and generic dark SaaS palettes.

### 5.3 Surface Colors

Public site:

- Mostly white or very light sky backgrounds.
- Navy headers and bands used intentionally.
- Real imagery provides warmth and specificity.

Member portal:

- Light operational surfaces.
- Member card can use navy for a premium, credential-like feel.
- Status and benefit accents should be clear.

Admin CRM:

- White and light gray surfaces.
- Navy used for navigation and primary actions.
- Status colors used consistently.

## 6. Typography Direction

The logo already has a bold uppercase geometric character. The product typography should support it without competing.

### 6.1 Recommended Font Pairing

Primary UI font:

- `Inter`, `Manrope`, or `Aptos`.

Display/accent font:

- `Sora`, `Space Grotesk`, or `Manrope`.

Recommended practical choice:

- Use `Inter` for the whole application.
- Use font weight, scale, and casing to create the brand feel.

Why:

- Inter is clean, readable, and strong for both member portal and admin CRM.
- It avoids looking too editorial or too decorative.
- It scales well from public pages to dense tables.

### 6.2 Type Rules

Headlines:

- Strong, concise, and grounded.
- Use sentence case or title case.
- Use uppercase only for short labels, chips, or member card details.

Body:

- Clear and direct.
- Avoid inflated marketing language.

Admin:

- Compact, readable, and information-first.
- Table text should not be oversized.

Letter spacing:

- Keep normal letter spacing for UI text.
- Use slightly wider uppercase only for small labels if needed.

### 6.3 Suggested Type Scale

| Token | Desktop | Mobile | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Display | 56px | 38px | Public hero only |
| H1 | 40px | 32px | Page titles |
| H2 | 30px | 26px | Section titles |
| H3 | 22px | 20px | Cards, panels |
| Body | 16px | 16px | Main text |
| Small | 14px | 14px | Metadata, tables |
| Micro | 12px | 12px | Labels, chips |

Rules:

- Do not use hero-scale type inside cards, admin panels, or compact dashboards.
- Admin table type should prioritize scanability over drama.

## 7. Layout Direction

### 7.1 Grid

Use a disciplined grid:

- Public content max width: 1120-1200px.
- Admin content: fluid, with comfortable table widths.
- Member portal: constrained enough to feel personal, wide enough for benefits and cards.

Spacing scale:

- 4px base.
- Common spacing: 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96.

### 7.2 Shape

Border radius:

- Small controls: 6px.
- Cards and panels: 8px maximum.
- Member card: 12px is acceptable because it represents a credential, not a generic UI card.

Rules:

- Avoid pill-shaped everything.
- Avoid cards inside cards.
- Use cards for repeated items, credentials, forms, and admin detail panels.
- Use full-width bands or unframed layouts for page sections.

### 7.3 Elevation

Use subtle elevation:

- Public benefit/tier cards can have light border and minimal shadow.
- Admin views should rely on borders and background contrast, not shadows.
- Member card can have slightly more depth.

Avoid:

- Heavy floating shadows.
- Glassmorphism.
- Layered translucent panels.

## 8. Imagery Direction

Imagery is critical. The platform should show real general aviation, not generic travel.

### 8.1 Best Image Subjects

Use:

- Small aircraft on ramps.
- GA cockpit details.
- Flight school scenes.
- Aerodrome runways.
- Hangar doors and aircraft maintenance context.
- Pilots and students in natural training moments.
- Club events around aircraft.
- Sponsor/partner locations.

Avoid:

- Airline jets.
- Anonymous clouds.
- Corporate office scenes.
- Stock pilots posing unnaturally.
- Dark, blurred, purely atmospheric images.
- Abstract aircraft illustrations as primary hero visuals.

### 8.2 Hero Imagery

The homepage hero should use a real or generated bitmap image that immediately signals general aviation.

Recommended hero directions:

1. Light aircraft parked near hangar at golden hour, with people nearby.
2. Small aircraft taxiing at a local aerodrome.
3. Cockpit/ramp scene showing practical flying preparation.

Hero rules:

- Image should show the actual GA world.
- Text should sit directly over or beside the image in a clean composition.
- Do not put hero copy inside a card.
- Do not use abstract gradients or SVG aircraft as the main hero.

### 8.3 Admin Imagery

Admin CRM should not use decorative imagery.

Use:

- Organization logos.
- Aircraft photos.
- Document thumbnails if useful.

Avoid:

- Hero images.
- Decorative banners.

## 9. Iconography Direction

Use simple line icons, preferably from Lucide or a similar consistent icon set.

Recommended icon themes:

- User/member.
- Credit card/payment.
- Badge/card.
- QR code.
- Building/organization.
- Handshake/contract.
- File/document.
- Mail/communication.
- Plane for aviation-specific items.
- Map pin/aerodrome.
- Calendar/renewal.
- Shield/security.

Rules:

- Icons should clarify actions, not decorate empty space.
- Use aviation icons sparingly.
- Do not manually draw one-off icons if a library icon exists.
- Buttons should use icons for common actions like edit, save, download, filter, search, upload, and delete.

## 10. Component Direction

### 10.1 Buttons

Primary button:

- Navy background.
- White text.
- Used for main actions: Join, Pay, Save, Publish.

Secondary button:

- White or light surface.
- Navy text.
- Border in panel line color.

Destructive button:

- Red or neutral button with red text, depending on risk.

Rules:

- One primary action per screen region.
- Admin tables should use compact icon buttons where appropriate.

### 10.2 Status Chips

Status chips are essential across the platform.

Examples:

- Active: green.
- Pending: amber.
- Expired: red or slate-red.
- Draft: gray.
- Archived: slate.
- Paid: green.
- Failed: red.

Rules:

- Color plus label, never color alone.
- Same status should look the same across member and admin views.

### 10.3 Cards

Use cards for:

- Membership tiers.
- Benefit previews.
- Member card.
- Dashboard summary items.
- Repeated entity records on mobile.

Do not use cards for:

- Entire page sections.
- Hero copy.
- Nested panels.

### 10.4 Tables

Admin tables should be calm and compact.

Required table features:

- Search.
- Filters.
- Sort where useful.
- Status chips.
- Row actions.
- Pagination.
- Empty state.

Table visual rules:

- Use clear column alignment.
- Use subtle row separators.
- Avoid zebra striping unless needed.
- Keep action buttons compact.

### 10.5 Forms

Forms should feel precise and trustworthy.

Rules:

- Group fields into logical sections.
- Required fields must be clear.
- Errors should appear next to fields.
- Save/cancel should be predictable.
- Long admin forms can use sticky footer actions.

### 10.6 Member Card

The digital member card is a brand-defining component.

Visual direction:

- Deep navy background.
- White logo or wordmark.
- Subtle technical linework or aircraft angle inspired by the logo.
- Strong member name.
- Clear tier and valid-until date.
- QR code in a clean white block.

Required card fields:

- Aeroskill Club branding.
- Member name.
- Member number.
- Membership tier.
- Valid until.
- Status.
- QR code.

Rules:

- Must be readable on mobile.
- Must work as a screenshot.
- Must not look like a payment card.
- Expired state must be obvious.

## 11. Public Website Direction

The public site should feel aspirational but grounded in real general aviation.

### 11.1 Home

Tone:

- Confident.
- Welcoming.
- Aviation-specific.

Visual structure:

- Strong first viewport with the Aeroskill Club name and GA image.
- Clear join CTA.
- Membership preview.
- Benefits preview.
- Partner credibility.

Avoid:

- Overly abstract hero sections.
- Empty metrics.
- Generic startup-style hero copy.

### 11.2 Membership

Tone:

- Clear and decision-oriented.

Visual structure:

- Three tier cards.
- Direct comparison table.
- Calm pricing display.
- Practical benefit language.

Tier visual emphasis:

- Explorer: light sky and navy.
- Pilot: navy and sky blue.
- Aviator Plus: navy with restrained amber accent.

### 11.3 Benefits

Tone:

- Useful and concrete.

Visual structure:

- Category filters.
- Benefit cards with provider, category, eligibility.
- Join/login prompt.

### 11.4 Partners

Tone:

- Ecosystem and credibility.

Visual structure:

- Partner categories.
- Logos/names in a clean grid.
- Partner inquiry CTA.

## 12. Member Portal Direction

The member portal should feel personal, useful, and calm.

Priorities:

- Status first.
- Card second.
- Benefits third.
- Payment/renewal clarity always visible.

Visual structure:

- Light app shell.
- Navy status/card accents.
- Clear dashboard cards.
- Benefit cards with eligibility labels.

Member dashboard hierarchy:

1. Membership status.
2. Valid until.
3. Digital card.
4. Featured benefits.
5. Alerts and recent activity.

Expired member state:

- Calm but unmistakable.
- Show renewal CTA.
- Limit benefit access according to product rules.

## 13. Admin CRM Direction

The admin CRM should feel like an aviation operations desk: organized, clear, and fast.

Tone:

- Efficient.
- Neutral.
- Low-drama.
- Data-first.

Visual structure:

- Sidebar navigation.
- Dense tables.
- Compact filters.
- Entity detail tabs.
- Status chips.
- Notes and document panels.

Avoid:

- Marketing page composition.
- Oversized cards.
- Decorative aircraft imagery.
- Unnecessary color.

Admin colors:

- Mostly white, slate, border gray.
- Navy for navigation and primary actions.
- Semantic colors only for state.

## 14. Content Voice

Voice:

- Direct.
- Practical.
- Aviation-aware.
- Warm, but not casual.

Use:

- "Join Aeroskill Club."
- "Your membership is active until..."
- "Show your member card."
- "Available for Pilot and Aviator Plus members."
- "Contract renewal due."

Avoid:

- "Unlock endless possibilities."
- "Take your journey to new heights."
- "Disrupting aviation communities."
- "World-class ecosystem platform."

Public copy should inspire trust. Member copy should help action. Admin copy should be concise.

## 15. Motion and Interaction

Motion should be minimal and purposeful.

Use:

- Subtle hover states.
- Smooth drawer transitions.
- Clear loading states.
- Payment processing progress.
- QR/card refresh feedback.

Avoid:

- Decorative scroll animations.
- Floating objects.
- Parallax-heavy hero sections.
- Overanimated dashboards.

## 16. Accessibility Direction

Requirements:

- Strong contrast for navy text and buttons.
- Status labels must include text, not only color.
- Forms must have labels and clear validation.
- Buttons must have visible focus states.
- QR validation page must work on mobile.
- Admin tables must remain usable with keyboard navigation.

Color caution:

- Amber on white may need dark text or stronger shade.
- Pale sky backgrounds must still support readable navy/slate text.

## 18. Do and Do Not

### Do

- Use the logo as the primary aviation signal.
- Use real GA imagery.
- Keep public pages confident and concrete.
- Make the member card feel like a premium credential.
- Make admin screens efficient and restrained.
- Use status chips consistently.
- Keep typography crisp and readable.
- Keep UI shapes modest and precise.

### Do Not

- Build a generic SaaS landing page.
- Use abstract gradient hero art.
- Fill the UI with airplane icons.
- Use airline or commercial travel imagery.
- Hide member status or renewal state.
- Make admin screens look like marketing pages.
- Overuse navy until the app feels heavy.
- Use amber as a broad brand color.
- Use vague motivational copy.

| --- | --- | --- |
| Public website | Aspirational, credible, GA-specific | Convert visitors and build trust |
| Member portal | Personal, clear, useful | Show status, card, benefits, renewal |
| Admin CRM | Calm, dense, operational | Manage members, partners, contracts, benefits |

## 20. Acceptance Criteria

The V1 design direction is successful when:

- The first screen clearly signals Aeroskill Club and general aviation.
- The visual system feels derived from the logo, not pasted onto it.
- Membership tiers are easy to compare.
- The digital member card feels trustworthy and scannable.
- Benefits feel practical and partner-backed.
- Admin CRM screens are efficient and not decorative.
- The platform feels modern without becoming corporate-generic.
